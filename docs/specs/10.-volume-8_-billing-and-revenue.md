# 1. Foundation: Why Billing Is a Major Workspace
Integrated Billing V2.0 is one of the largest VistA packages by functional scope. The September 2025 User Guide has over 300 pages documenting 100+ terminal options organized across five major menus. IB moved from 'a package with the singular purpose of identifying billable episodes of care and creating bills, to a package responsible for the whole billing process through to the passing of charges to Accounts Receivable.' (IB User Guide Preface)
IB contains FIVE major functional modules:
(1) Patient Billing — copayments, means-test-based charges for VA patients, category A/B/C billing, billing clocks, charge cancellation/editing
(2) Third-Party Billing — insurance claims creation, EDI transmission to payers via FSC, remittance processing, claim lifecycle tracking, bill authorization
(3) Insurance Data Capture — patient insurance policy entry/editing, insurance company management, insurance verification (eIV), insurance buffer processing
(4) Claims Tracking — episode-based tracking of potentially billable cases, utilization review, insurance review coordination, appeals/denials management
(5) Encounter Form Utilities — encounter form design, clinic assignment, charge capture form management
Additionally, IB has specialized sub-modules added by recent patches: Urgent Care Visit Tracking (IB*2*663, March 2020), Mental Health Copay Management (IB*2*784, March 2024), Cleland-Dole Act copay cancellation, COMPACT Act compliance, Former OTH Patient processing, and Oracle Health/Cerner copay accumulator integration (IB*2*769, August 2025).
CRITICAL NON-VA WARNING: VA Integrated Billing is built around VA-specific eligibility categories: Service-Connected %, NSC (Non-Service-Connected), Means Test categories A/B/C/G, CHAMPVA, Medicare, TRICARE, Medal of Honor, AI/AN (American Indian/Alaska Native) attestation, Purple Heart, Former OTH (Other Than Honorable) discharge, Urgent Care visit tracking per MISSION Act 2018, Community Care, Cleland-Dole Act mental health exemptions, COMPACT Act. For international/commercial deployment, the billing workspace requires OVERLAY LOGIC for local payer systems, billing codes, claims formats, and revenue cycle rules. IB provides the ENGINE framework but payer-specific rules MUST be overlays.

## 1.4 Existing RPCs
IB has ZERO known RPCs for GUI billing operations. All billing workflows are terminal-driven. The eIV interface operates through HL7 messaging, not RPCs. The EDI claims transmission operates through IB routines that generate HL7 messages to the FSC, not through RPCs.
The ONLY existing RPCs that touch billing data are indirect:

Encounter/PCE data feeds the billing charge capture process. Charges are derived from encounter data.

Save encounter data: diagnoses (ICD-10), procedures (CPT), providers.

N/A — HL7 event processing
VistA event drivers (MAS Movement, Means Test, Scheduling Check-In) automatically trigger billing actions. IB TM: 'The MAS Movement Event Driver invokes this routine. The Scheduling Check-In Event Driver invokes this routine.'
Many billing charges are auto-generated by events (admission, check-in, means test change), not by manual user action.
CRITICAL ARCHITECTURE NOTE: Many IB charges are AUTO-GENERATED by VistA event drivers — not by manual user action. When a patient checks in for an appointment, the Scheduling Check-In Event Driver triggers IB copay charge creation. When a patient is admitted, the MAS Movement Event Driver triggers IB processing. The modern UI must NOT try to replicate this event-driven auto-billing — it should continue using VistA's event infrastructure. The modern UI manages the REVIEW, EDITING, SUBMISSION, and TRACKING of charges and claims that VistA's event system creates.

# 5. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: VA billing eligibility categories (SC%, Means Test A/B/C/G, CHAMPVA, Medicare, TRICARE, MOH, AI/AN, Purple Heart, Former OTH, Urgent Care MISSION Act, Cleland-Dole Act, COMPACT Act) are VA-specific. Non-VA tenants need overlay logic for local payer systems.
Rule 2: Many charges are AUTO-GENERATED by VistA event drivers (check-in, admission, means test change). The modern UI manages review/editing/submission/tracking of charges, NOT initial charge creation for these auto-generated charges.
Rule 3: EDI claim submission goes through FSC middleware via IB routines (IBXS*/IBXSC*/IBXX*), NOT directly to payers. The modern UI triggers the IB EDI process but does not implement EDI protocol.
Rule 4: Insurance verification (eIV) is a background HL7 process. Display verification results but do not replicate the HL7 inquiry mechanism.
Rule 5: TPJI must provide cross-navigation between all 17 data sections from any section. The billing clerk must never lose patient/bill context when moving between sections.
Rule 6: Bill authorization requires the IB AUTHORIZE key. Unauthorized users can prepare bills but cannot approve them for transmission.
Rule 7: Charge cancellation requires a structured reason from CHARGE REMOVE REASON #350.3. New reasons are added by patches (PANDEMIC RESPONSE, UC-specific reasons, etc.) — the dropdown source must be dynamic from #350.3, not hard-coded.
Rule 8: Bill formats: 837P/CMS-1500 for professional claims, 837I/UB-04 for institutional claims. The claim format is determined by bill type, not user choice.
Rule 9: Timely filing limits vary by payer and are stored on INSURANCE COMPANY #36. The UI should calculate and display days remaining for unbilled/untransmitted claims.
Rule 10: Patient eligibility data (means test category, SC%, priority group, special statuses) affects charge amounts and billing paths. The billing UI must display current eligibility prominently when working charges or claims.
Rule 11: The Insurance Buffer is a staging area. Data entering the buffer must be reviewed and accepted before becoming the patient's insurance record. Never auto-accept buffer entries without review.
Rule 12: Patients with Category 1 High Risk for Suicide flags require special handling during charge operations per IB*2*614. The modern UI must check for this flag.
Rule 13: Urgent Care visit tracking is a MISSION Act requirement tracking free visit eligibility based on enrollment priority group at time of visit. Multi-facility synchronization is required. IB*2*663 through IB*2*677 implement this.
Rule 14: Mental Health copay management per Cleland-Dole Act of 2022 and COMPACT Act is implemented by IB*2*784 and IB*2*760. This is an active area of development with patches as recent as January 2025 (IB*2*779).
Rule 15: The Automated Biller runs as a background TaskManager job. Provide monitoring visibility but do not replace TaskManager scheduling.
