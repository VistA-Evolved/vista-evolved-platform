# 1. Foundation: The Collection Side of Revenue
Volume 8 covered Integrated Billing (IB) — the creation of bills from clinical encounters. This supplement covers Accounts Receivable (AR) — what happens AFTER the bill is created: collecting payment, processing remittances, managing delinquent debts, issuing refunds, and reconciling with the financial management system. Without AR, you can generate bills but have no mechanism to collect on them, track payments, or manage the revenue cycle to completion.
The AR lifecycle begins where IB ends:
IB creates the bill (UB-04/CMS-1500 format) -> IB passes the bill to AR via the PRCASVC routine (SETUP^PRCASVC creates the #430 record, REL^PRCASVC releases it to Fiscal) -> AR audits, activates, and transmits to FMS -> AR manages the full collection lifecycle: statements, follow-up, payments, adjustments, write-offs, DMC referral, Treasury offset.

# 6. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: AR is the FINANCIAL COUNTERPART to IB. IB creates bills; AR collects on them. They share files (#399 BILL/CLAIMS, #399.3 RATE TYPE) and AR is tightly integrated with IB. The modern UI must maintain this integration — bills must flow seamlessly from IB creation to AR collection.
Rule 2: Every AR financial transaction MUST create an audit trail in #430 transaction history. Payments, adjustments, write-offs, refunds, interest charges, and administrative costs are all recorded as transactions with type (#430.3), amount, date, and user. This audit trail is subject to federal financial audit.
Rule 3: The PRCA NIGHTLY PROCESS is essential. It runs 34+ jobs at 2am including: bill activation, interest calculation, statement generation, DMC referral checks, FMS transmission, ePayments auto-posting, and auto-auditing. The modern UI must not interfere with the nightly process and should provide monitoring visibility.
Rule 4: ePayments (EDI Lockbox) is the highest-volume AR workflow. A busy facility processes hundreds of ERAs daily. The ERA worklist must be performant, support auto-posting for matched ERAs, and provide efficient exception handling for unmatched claims.
Rule 5: ERA/835 processing uses HIPAA-standard Claim Adjustment Reason Codes (CARCs) and Remittance Advice Remark Codes (RARCs) to explain payment differences. The modern UI must display CARCs/RARCs with their standard descriptions (e.g. CARC 1 = 'Deductible Amount', CARC 2 = 'Coinsurance Amount', CARC 45 = 'Charges exceed your contracted/legislated fee arrangement').
Rule 6: First Party copayment billing is driven by patient eligibility (Means Test category, service-connected percentage). Copay amounts are calculated by IB at check-in/admission/prescription fill. AR manages the collection of those copayments via CCPC statements and, if delinquent, DMC referral.
Rule 7: DMC referral is AUTOMATIC for debts meeting criteria (> 120 days delinquent, above minimum amount). The monthly referral check runs as part of the nightly process on the last Thursday of each month. DMC UG: 'system builds Master File mail message and transmits to AITC.' The modern UI should provide visibility into DMC referral status and allow authorized staff to manage DMC lesser amounts.
Rule 8: FMS (Financial Management System) integration is mandatory for federal accounting. Every bill activation, payment, write-off, and refund generates FMS documents transmitted to Austin. If FMS transmission fails, the AR UM provides regeneration options. The modern UI must provide FMS status monitoring.
Rule 9: Write-off authority is governed: first-party debts can be written off by the fiscal officer or CPAC fiscal officer per established criteria. Third-party debts require Office of General Counsel (OGC) approval. The modern UI must enforce write-off authorization levels.
Rule 10: Refund processing requires careful handling: overpayments must be identified (nightly process sets to REFUND REVIEW), refund amounts calculated, and refund documents generated. VHA Directive 1599(1) has specific rules about refunds when both patient and TPP have paid.
Rule 11: INTERNATIONAL ADAPTATION: VA AR is deeply wired to US federal financial systems (FMS, CCPC, DMC, Treasury, PNC Bank). For international deployment, the billing overlay adapter must provide equivalent functions: local financial system integration (instead of FMS), local statement generation (instead of CCPC), local debt collection (instead of DMC), local payment processing (instead of PNC/Treasury). The AR data structures (#430 bills, #340 debtors, transaction history) are universal — the external integrations change.
Rule 12: ePayments CARC/RARC data is uploaded from FSC. ePayments UM (Dec 2025): 'Updates the VistA upload process of CARC/RARC data sent by FSC.' The modern UI must support CARC/RARC display and use standard industry descriptions.
Rule 13: Deposit ticket management links AR receipts to bank deposits. Agent Cashier confirms bank deposits by entering bank trace number and deposit date. This reconciliation is a financial control requirement.
Rule 14: AR reports are critical for financial management: the 215 Report (standard VA financial report), aged receivables (how old are outstanding debts), collection rate analysis, DMC referral tracking, ePayments audit reports. The modern UI must provide these reports with appropriate drill-down capability.
