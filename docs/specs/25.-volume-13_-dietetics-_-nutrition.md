# 1. Foundation: Dual-Function Nutrition System
Dietetics V5.5 (Nutrition and Food Service — NFS) is unique among VistA packages because it serves TWO distinct operational domains that intersect at the patient: (1) Clinical Dietetics — the clinical assessment and ordering side, where Registered Dietitian Nutritionists (RDNs) evaluate patients, create nutrition care plans, and enter diet orders, and (2) Food Management — the food production and service side, where food service staff prepare meals, manage recipes, track inventory, and deliver trays to patients based on their diet orders.
Source: NFS UM: 'Clinical Dietetics activities of nutrition screening, nutrition assessment, diet order entry, tubefeeding and supplemental feeding orders, patient food preferences, specific diet pattern calculations, nutrient analysis of meals, consult reporting, encounter tracking, and quality care monitoring. Complete automation of food production activities, service and distribution, inventory and cost management, recipe expansion, menu and recipe nutrient analysis, meal and diet pattern development, diet card and tray ticket printing.'

Nurse or Dietetic Technician at admission/encounter

Nutrition screening via clinical reminders (Vol 4 Supplement) or NFS screening tools. VHA Directive 1438: 'nutrition screening within 24 hours of admission.'
## 2. Nutrition Assessment
Registered Dietitian Nutritionist (RDN)
Comprehensive nutrition assessment: anthropometric data (height, weight, BMI), biochemical data (lab values — albumin, prealbumin, glucose, renal panel), clinical data (medical history, medications, GI function), dietary data (intake history, food preferences, allergies). Calculate nutrient needs (calories, protein, fluid). VHA Directive 1438: 'RDN conducting nutrition assessment.'
NFS Assessment functions. DIETS #115 patient dietary record. Lab values from Lab (Vol 6). Vitals from Clinical (Vol 4).
## 3. Nutrition Diagnosis
RDN
Identify nutrition problems using Nutrition Care Process (NCP) standardized terminology: e.g. 'Inadequate oral intake related to dysphagia as evidenced by 10% weight loss in 3 months.'
NFS Assessment documentation. Stored in patient dietary record.
## 4. Nutrition Intervention / Diet Order
RDN or Physician
Enter diet order: regular diet, therapeutic diet (cardiac, renal, diabetic, dysphagia), texture modification (pureed, minced, soft), fluid restriction, tubefeeding order, supplemental feeding order, parenteral nutrition (coordinated with pharmacy). NFS UM: 'Diet Order Entry information can be used by Physicians, Nurses, and MAS personnel.'
DIETS #115 diet order fields. Diet order integrates with CPRS ordering (Vol 4). Tubefeeding/supplemental feeding orders.
## 5. Meal Production & Service
Food Service Staff
Kitchen receives diet orders via tray tickets/diet cards. Meals prepared per recipes accounting for diet modifications and food preferences. Trays assembled and delivered to patients. NFS UM: 'diet card and tray ticket printing.'
NFS Food Management: RECIPE files, INGREDIENT files, PRODUCTION FACILITY, SERVICE POINT, tray ticket generation.
## 6. Monitoring & Evaluation
RDN
Monitor patient nutrition status: intake adequacy, weight trends, lab values, tolerance of diet/feeding. Adjust diet order as needed. Document encounters. VHA Directive 1438: 'RDN must monitor nutrition diagnoses, goals, and care plan progress.'
DIETITIAN TICKLER FILE (follow-up reminders). NFS encounter tracking. DIETETIC CONSULTS.

## 3.2 NFS-05: Food Preferences / Allergy Integration
### Purpose
Manages the critical link between the allergy system (GMR ALLERGIES #120.82) and the food service system (FOOD PREFERENCES #115.2). When a food-type allergy is entered in the clinical allergy system (Vol 4), an automatic preference exclusion is created in the dietetics system so that the allergen is excluded from the patient's tray ticket. This is a PATIENT SAFETY integration.

# 5. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: FOOD ALLERGY TO TRAY TICKET FLOW IS PATIENT SAFETY CRITICAL. When a food-type allergy is entered in the clinical allergy system (#120.82), it MUST automatically create an exclusion preference in #115.2, and that exclusion MUST appear on the tray ticket. This is the single most important data flow in the NFS package. A tray with a peanut-containing item delivered to a patient with peanut allergy can be fatal. The modern UI must make this integration visible and verifiable.
Rule 2: Diet orders for dysphagia patients are HIGH-RISK safety orders. Use the International Dysphagia Diet Standardisation Initiative (IDDSI) framework for standardized texture levels (Levels 0-7). The modern UI should prominently display IDDSI level on the diet order and tray ticket. Changing a dysphagia diet order should require explicit confirmation with the IDDSI level clearly shown.
Rule 3: Nutrition assessment integrates with multiple other workspaces: Vitals (Vol 4 — weight, height, BMI), Laboratory (Vol 6 — albumin, prealbumin, glucose, electrolytes, renal panel), Clinical (Vol 4 — problem list for medical conditions affecting nutrition), Pharmacy (Vol 5 — medications affecting nutrition, drug-nutrient interactions). The assessment screen must pull data from these sources, not require re-entry.
Rule 4: Tubefeeding orders are SEPARATE from regular diet orders. A patient may have: (a) NPO diet order (nothing by mouth) + tubefeeding order (nutrition via tube), or (b) a modified oral diet + supplemental tubefeeding. The modern UI must support both states simultaneously and clearly display both on the patient's nutrition profile.
Rule 5: Supplemental feedings (oral nutrition supplements like Ensure, Boost; between-meal nourishments) are ordered by RDNs and tracked separately from meals. VHA Directive 1438: 'ONS ordering practices must align with RDN assessment and goals.' The modern UI should track supplement intake and compliance.
Rule 6: Food production and service (NFS-08 through NFS-12) is a FOOD SERVICE OPERATION, not a clinical operation. The users are food service workers, cooks, and supervisors — NOT clinical staff. The UI for food management pages should be optimized for kitchen/production environment: large text, simple layouts, touch-friendly for kitchen tablets, high contrast for readability in busy kitchen conditions.
Rule 7: The Nutrition Care Process (NCP) is the international standard framework for nutrition care: Assessment -> Diagnosis -> Intervention -> Monitoring & Evaluation (ADIME). The modern UI should support structured NCP documentation with standardized terminology (IDNT — International Dietetics and Nutrition Terminology).
Rule 8: DRI (Dietary Reference Intakes) values are published by the National Academies and updated periodically. The DRI VALUES file must be maintained and used for nutrient adequacy comparisons. Different DRI values apply based on age, sex, pregnancy/lactation status.
Rule 9: Recipe and menu management is a COMPLEX food service function. Recipe expansion (scaling a recipe from 10 servings to 100 servings) must correctly adjust all ingredient quantities. Nutrient analysis per serving must be calculated from ingredient nutrient data. This is a computational function that the modern UI must support with real-time calculation display.
Rule 10: INTERNATIONAL ADAPTATION: Diet types and food preferences are HIGHLY culture-specific. Kosher, Halal, Hindu vegetarian, Buddhist vegetarian, and other cultural dietary practices vary by region. The IDDSI framework for dysphagia diets is internationally recognized. Nutrient requirements (DRI) are published by different countries (US: National Academies, UK: SACN, EU: EFSA). The NFS configuration must support locale-specific diet patterns, food catalogs, and nutrient reference values.
Rule 11: Outpatient nutrition services (counseling, medical nutrition therapy — MNT) require encounter documentation for billing (CPT codes: 97802 initial MNT, 97803 re-assessment, 97804 group MNT). The NFS encounter tracking must integrate with billing (Vol 8) for outpatient nutrition services.
Rule 12: The Tenant Profile Matrix determines NFS visibility: Profile A (Full Hospital) = PRIMARY (full inpatient + outpatient NFS). Profile B (Multi-Specialty Clinic) = OPTIONAL (outpatient nutrition counseling only if offered). Profiles C-G = typically HIDDEN (standalone pharmacy/lab/imaging don't need NFS). Profile D (Standalone Pharmacy) might need supplement tracking as an exception.
