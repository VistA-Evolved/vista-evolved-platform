# 1. Foundation: Behavioral Health in VistA
The Mental Health package (YS namespace) provides standardized psychological and behavioral health assessment instrument administration, scoring, and tracking. It does NOT replace the clinical documentation in TIU (progress notes, treatment plans) or the order entry in CPRS (medication orders, consults). Instead, it provides the MEASUREMENT-BASED CARE (MBC) layer: standardized instruments administered before, during, and after treatment to measure patient outcomes objectively.
VHA's MBC model is Collect-Share-Act: (1) Collect — administer standardized instruments (PHQ-9, GAD-7, PCL-5, etc.) at each encounter. (2) Share — present scores and trends to the clinician and patient together. (3) Act — use measurement data to inform treatment decisions (adjust medication, change therapy approach, step up/down care).

## 1.3 Mental Health Assistant (MHA)
MHA is the modern web application for mental health assessment administration. It replaced the older CPRS-embedded assessment tools. MHA UM (Dec 2025): 'MHA provides clinicians with a singular point for assessment assignment and report integrated with CPRS/VistA systems.' MHA supports: staff-administered assessments (clinician reads questions, enters responses), patient-facing assessments (patient completes on VA Device — tablet/kiosk), remote assessments (patient completes on personal device via Mental Health Checkup mobile app), and CAT (Computerized Adaptive Testing) for efficient assessment.
MHA features documented in Dec 2025 UM: Create Assessment, Create Assignments (for patient self-entry), Staff Entry Execution Screen, Veteran's Device (MHA4V) patient-facing entry, Review Remote Assessment Results Dashboard, Create Progress Notes (from assessment data), Download/Export CSV, View Report, Send Feedback to Veteran, CAT (Computerized Adaptive Testing with language selection).

# 5. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: PHQ-9 ITEM 9 IS A PATIENT SAFETY GATE. Any positive response (score > 0) on Item 9 ('Thoughts that you would be better off dead or of hurting yourself in some way') MUST trigger an immediate clinical safety protocol. The modern UI must: (a) display a prominent, non-dismissible alert, (b) require the clinician to document a follow-up action (C-SSRS administered, safety plan created, crisis intervention initiated), (c) prevent the assessment from being 'completed' without addressing Item 9. This is not optional — it is a patient safety and regulatory requirement.
Rule 2: Assessment scores must be AUTO-CALCULATED from item responses per the scoring algorithm defined in MH TESTS AND SURVEYS #601.71. The clinician should NEVER manually calculate or enter a total score. Auto-scoring eliminates calculation errors and ensures consistent interpretation.
Rule 3: Measurement-Based Care (MBC) requires TREND VISUALIZATION. The score trend graph (MH-04) is not a nice-to-have — it is the core of the MBC 'Share' step. Clinicians and patients must be able to see how scores change over treatment. The graph must overlay treatment events (medication changes, therapy start/stop) to enable correlation analysis.
Rule 4: MHA (Mental Health Assistant) is the VA's EXISTING modern web tool for assessment administration. VistA Evolved's MH workspace should either integrate with MHA (if deployed at the facility) or provide equivalent functionality. MHA supports: staff entry, patient self-entry (MHA4V on VA tablet), remote entry (Mental Health Checkup mobile app), and CAT (Computerized Adaptive Testing). All these modalities should be supported.
Rule 5: Assessment results stored in MH ADMINISTRATION #601.84 must be IMMUTABLE once completed (per Audit/Irreversibility Matrix: C/L3 — compensating/legal record). A completed assessment cannot be deleted; if an error occurred, an amendment note is added. The original item responses and score are preserved as the legal record of the clinical assessment.
Rule 6: Mental health diagnoses use ICD-10-CM F-codes (Mental, Behavioral, and Neurodevelopmental Disorders: F01-F99). The diagnosis entry screen must support: DSM-5 diagnostic criteria alignment, provisional vs confirmed status, multiple concurrent diagnoses (comorbidity is the norm in mental health), and diagnostic formulation narrative.
Rule 7: Group therapy management is a UNIQUE mental health workflow not found in other clinical workspaces. A therapy group has: a defined membership (patients enrolled), a facilitator (therapist), a schedule (weekly sessions), attendance tracking (present, absent, excused), and group progress notes (separate from individual notes). The modern UI must support group-level documentation alongside individual patient documentation.
Rule 8: Clinical reminders (Vol 4 Supplement) drive mental health screening in primary care: PHQ-2 annually for depression screening, PC-PTSD-5 annually for PTSD screening, AUDIT-C annually for alcohol screening. The MH workspace must show screening compliance status and provide quick access to administer overdue screens.
Rule 9: CRISIS INTERVENTION: The suicide risk assessment (MH-05) and safety planning functionality must be INSTANTLY accessible from any MH screen. A clinician should be able to initiate a C-SSRS and safety plan in one click, without navigating through menus. This is an emergency workflow — speed matters.
Rule 10: Progress notes generated from assessment data (MHA: 'Create Progress Note') must include: instrument name, administration date, total score, severity interpretation, and optionally individual item responses. The note is filed as a TIU document (#8925) and appears in the patient chart alongside other clinical notes.
Rule 11: CONFIDENTIALITY: Mental health records may have additional privacy protections beyond standard medical records. In the US: 42 CFR Part 2 (Confidentiality of Substance Use Disorder Patient Records) restricts disclosure of substance use disorder treatment records. The modern UI must support segmented access controls where mental health and substance use records have stricter sharing rules than general medical records. The Security/Permission Matrix (Phase 2, Artifact 6) must account for this.
Rule 12: INTERNATIONAL ADAPTATION: The assessment instruments (PHQ-9, GAD-7, PCL-5) are INTERNATIONALLY VALIDATED and used worldwide. They have been translated into dozens of languages. The instrument definitions in #601.71 should support multi-language versions of each instrument, selectable per patient language preference (MHA Dec 2025 UM: 'CAT Language' selection). Scoring algorithms are universal — the same PHQ-9 scoring applies regardless of language.
