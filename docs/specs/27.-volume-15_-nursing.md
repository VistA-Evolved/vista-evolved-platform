# 1. Foundation: Nursing in VistA
Nursing documentation in VistA is DISTRIBUTED across multiple packages rather than contained in a single monolithic Nursing module. This distribution reflects nursing's cross-cutting role — nurses interact with nearly every clinical system. The modern UI must UNIFY these distributed functions into a coherent nursing workspace.

## 1.2 PADP Architecture
The Patient Assessment Documentation Package (PADP, NUPA 1.0) is the standardized nursing assessment system. PADP TM: 'The PADP software application enables Registered Nurses (RNs) to document, in a standardized format, patient care during an inpatient stay. Content is standardized for use across the VA system, with some site-configurable parameters.'
PADP consists of: (1) A KIDS build (NUPA 1.0) installed on VistA, (2) Delphi GUI templates in executables launched from CPRS Tools menu, (3) Integration with TIU (progress notes generated from assessment data), PCE (encounter data), Health Factors, and 14 VistA files + 36 parameters. PADP TM: 'PADP adds to VistA new health factors, fourteen files, thirty-six parameters, and five security keys.' Assessments include: RN Admission Assessment (comprehensive), RN Reassessment (shift-based), and Nursing Data Collection.
PADP's Delphi GUI executables are LEGACY technology. VistA Evolved must replace these with modern web-based nursing assessment forms that produce the same TIU progress notes and health factor data. The assessment CONTENT (questions, scoring, required fields) should be preserved; the delivery mechanism changes from Delphi EXE to web forms.
## 1.3 Intake & Output System
The I&O application documents all patient fluid intake and output during an inpatient stay. VistA Monograph: 'Users may electronically document patient intake (oral fluids, tube feedings, IV fluids, irrigations) and output (urine, NG secretions, emesis, drainage, liquid feces/stool). Intake data entered through quick or detailed route. Quick route documents total fluid consumed. Detailed route requests specific type of fluid intake along with quantity.' I&O data is critical for: fluid balance calculation, kidney function monitoring, post-surgical management, heart failure management, and burn care.

Total score: <= 9 Very High Risk, 10-12 High Risk, 13-14 Moderate Risk, 15-18 Mild Risk, >= 19 No Risk. Triggers: pressure injury prevention interventions (repositioning schedule, pressure-relieving mattress, skin inspection). CRITICAL regulatory documentation per Joint Commission and CMS.
RN, Physician (order)

# 3. Screen Contracts
## 3.1 NUR-01: Nurse Dashboard / Patient Assignment Board
### Purpose
The nurse's HOME SCREEN — the first thing they see when they log in. Shows all patients assigned to this nurse with real-time status indicators for everything that needs attention: overdue vital signs, medications due or overdue, new orders to review, new lab results, active safety alerts (fall risk, isolation), tasks due, and I&O status. This replaces the paper nursing assignment sheet and the manual process of checking each patient's chart individually.

DESIGN NOTE: The Nurse Dashboard must be optimized for MOBILE (tablet) use. Nurses carry tablets on rounds. The dashboard must be readable at arm's length, support touch interaction, and update in near-real-time (WebSocket or polling). It is the MOST-USED screen in the entire nursing workspace. Every design decision should prioritize this page.

# 5. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: The Nurse Dashboard (NUR-01) is the MOST IMPORTANT screen in the nursing workspace. It must be the HOME SCREEN for all nurse roles in Profile A (Full Hospital) and Profile E (Inpatient Pharmacy/BCMA). It must update in near-real-time, be optimized for tablet use (touch-friendly, readable at arm's length), and aggregate data from at least 8 VistA packages (vitals, BCMA, orders, lab, I&O, health factors, patient record flags, TIU).
Rule 2: I&O documentation (NUR-04) is a HIGH-FREQUENCY entry screen. Nurses may enter I&O data 10-20+ times per shift per patient. The UI must support RAPID data entry: common volumes as quick-select buttons, auto-population from IV orders and tube feedings, minimal click/tap count, large touch targets for tablet use. Every extra tap costs thousands of hours of nursing time across a facility.
Rule 3: PADP's Delphi GUI executables must be replaced with modern web-based nursing assessment forms. The assessment CONTENT (questions, scoring algorithms, required fields) should be preserved from PADP's standardized templates. The assessment output should still generate TIU progress notes (for CPRS chart display) and Health Factors (for clinical reminders and quality reporting). PADP TM: 'generates progress notes linked to PCE encounter data.'
Rule 4: Fall Risk (Morse Scale) and Skin Integrity (Braden Scale) assessments are PATIENT SAFETY requirements per Joint Commission and CMS. They must be completed on admission, at defined intervals (per facility policy — typically Q shift or Q daily), on transfer, and on change in condition. The modern UI should trigger automated reminders when assessments are overdue and display risk levels prominently on the nurse dashboard and in the patient header across ALL workspaces.
Rule 5: Restraint documentation (NUR-08) is one of the MOST REGULATED nursing documentation requirements. CMS Conditions of Participation and Joint Commission standards specify: physician order required, order renewal timeframe (Q24H for non-behavioral health, Q4H for behavioral health), patient monitoring frequency (Q2H checks: circulation, range of motion, nutrition, hydration, elimination), documentation of continued need, patient/family notification. The modern UI must enforce all regulatory timeframes with alerts for overdue renewals and monitoring checks.
Rule 6: Nursing handoff (NUR-10 SBAR) is a PATIENT SAFETY initiative. Structured handoff communication reduces errors during shift transitions. The handoff report should be AUTO-GENERATED from current patient data (vitals trends, I&O totals, active orders, pending results, active safety concerns, recent assessments) with the nurse adding a brief narrative for Situation and Recommendation. The system-generated content ensures nothing is missed.
Rule 7: Nursing care plans (NUR-07) use standardized nursing terminologies: NANDA-I for nursing diagnoses, NOC for outcomes, NIC for interventions. These are internationally recognized frameworks. The modern UI should provide pre-built care plan templates by common nursing diagnoses (e.g. Risk for Falls, Impaired Skin Integrity, Acute Pain, Ineffective Airway Clearance) that the nurse can select and customize.
Rule 8: Staffing and acuity (NUR-11) must support the VHA's evidence-based staffing methodology. Patient acuity classification drives staffing recommendations. The charge nurse uses the staffing dashboard to make real-time assignment decisions: which nurse gets which patients based on acuity, competency, and workload balance. The dashboard should display current and projected staffing needs.
Rule 9: The nursing workspace must integrate tightly with: Clinical Workspace (Vol 4 — notes, orders, results), BCMA (Vol 5C — medication administration), Vitals (Vol 4 — vital signs entry), NFS (Vol 13 — diet orders, I&O for tube feedings), Pharmacy (Vol 5/5B — medication orders for the medication column on the dashboard), Lab (Vol 6 — results for the results column). The nurse dashboard is fundamentally a CROSS-WORKSPACE AGGREGATION tool.
Rule 10: Patient education documentation (NUR-09) should use the TEACH-BACK method as best practice: after teaching, the patient demonstrates understanding by explaining back in their own words. The modern UI should support: education topic selection (from standardized library), delivery method, teach-back assessment (verbalized understanding, return demonstration, needs reinforcement), and barriers documentation (language, literacy, cognitive, sensory).
Rule 11: INTERNATIONAL ADAPTATION: Nursing assessments, care plans, and documentation are highly UNIVERSAL. The Morse Fall Scale and Braden Scale are used internationally. NANDA-I, NOC, and NIC are international standards. I&O documentation is universal. Staffing models vary by country (nurse-to-patient ratios regulated differently), but the acuity-based staffing concept is universal. What may change: regulatory documentation requirements (CMS/Joint Commission rules are US-specific; other countries have equivalent but different requirements).
Rule 12: Tenant Profile Matrix visibility: Nursing workspace = PRIMARY for Profile A (Full Hospital) and Profile E (Inpatient Pharmacy/BCMA — nurses use BCMA and the dashboard). OPTIONAL for Profile B (Multi-Specialty Clinic — outpatient nursing documentation). HIDDEN for Profiles C, D, F, G (not inpatient care).
