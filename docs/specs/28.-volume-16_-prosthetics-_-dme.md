# 1. Foundation: VA Prosthetic & Sensory Aids Service
VA PSAS (Prosthetic & Sensory Aids Service) provides one of the most comprehensive prosthetic/DME benefit programs in the world. Unlike commercial insurance which limits DME coverage, the VA provides any medically necessary prosthetic device or service to eligible veterans. PSAS: 'provides a full range of equipment and services ranging from items worn by the veteran (artificial limb, hearing aid), to items that improve accessibility (ramps, vehicle modifications), to devices surgically placed (hips, pacemakers).'

# 5. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: HCPCS codes are the UNIVERSAL item identification system for prosthetics. Every prosthetic item issued is coded with a HCPCS code (E-codes for DME, K-codes for wheelchairs, L-codes for orthotics/prosthetics, V-codes for vision/hearing). The HCPCS code drives: national reporting (NPPD), billing (for non-VA facilities), contract compliance tracking, and utilization analysis. The item search (PR-03) must provide efficient HCPCS lookup with code + description + PSAS HCPCS mapping.
Rule 2: The Patient Prosthetic Record (PR-02 / 2319) is the MOST ACCESSED screen in the prosthetics workspace. Every prosthetic interaction starts with reviewing the patient's equipment history: what do they currently have, when was it issued, is it due for replacement, what repairs have been done. This screen must load quickly and present a clear, organized view of potentially decades of prosthetic transactions.
Rule 3: Home Oxygen (PR-06) is the HIGHEST ONGOING COST category in prosthetics due to monthly rental/resupply. It is also the highest PATIENT SAFETY concern because oxygen-dependent patients can die without their equipment. The home oxygen management screen must support: prescription tracking, vendor delivery coordination, compliance monitoring (is the patient using the equipment?), resupply scheduling, and emergency notification (as demonstrated during Hurricane Helene when VA tracked all home oxygen patients).
Rule 4: Prosthetic purchasing integrates with IFCAP (Integrated Funds Control, Accounting and Procurement — Vol 9 Supply Chain). Purchase orders created in Prosthetics generate obligations in IFCAP File #442. The modern UI must maintain this integration — prosthetic purchases are federal procurement actions subject to FAR (Federal Acquisition Regulation) and VA acquisition policy. Pros TM: 'entries added must be in accordance with IFCAP naming standards.'
Rule 5: NPPD reporting is MANDATORY for all VA prosthetic transactions. Every item issued, repaired, or rented must be reported to the National Prosthetics Patient Database. NPPD data drives: national utilization analysis, contract negotiation (VA leverages aggregate purchasing data for better pricing), quality monitoring, and congressional reporting. The modern UI should validate NPPD data completeness at transaction entry time, not after the fact.
Rule 6: Prosthetic ELIGIBILITY varies by item category and veteran status. Hearing aids require specific eligibility (SC for hearing loss, Purple Heart, POW, etc.). Eyeglasses have their own eligibility rules. HISA has lifetime dollar limits ($6,800 SC / $2,000 NSC). The eligibility screen (PR-07) must check multiple eligibility criteria and clearly display what the veteran qualifies for, preventing inappropriate issuance.
Rule 7: Complex devices (artificial limbs, power wheelchairs, custom orthotics) follow the 2529-3 WORKFLOW (PR-08): clinical evaluation -> device specification -> procurement/fabrication -> fitting -> adjustment -> delivery -> follow-up. This is a multi-week or multi-month process. The workflow tracking screen must show current status, next action required, and aging alerts for stalled orders.
Rule 8: The Prosthetic Item Master must support VA's national contract structure: BPA (Blanket Purchase Agreement) items are on mandatory national contract with pre-negotiated pricing. Waiver items are BPA items purchased from a non-contract vendor (requires documented justification). Excluded items are not covered by any BPA. Pros GUI UM: 'BPA/Contract prescribed because it meets needs. Excluded means item not covered by National Prosthetics Mandatory BPA/Contract.' The purchasing workflow must enforce BPA compliance.
Rule 9: Prosthetics CANCELLATION requires supervisor notification. When a purchase order or transaction is cancelled, VistA sends a bulletin to the RMPR CANCEL TRANSACTION mail group. Pros TM: 'bulletin advises Prosthetics supervisors whenever a transaction is cancelled.' The modern UI must maintain this audit trail and notification.
Rule 10: Vehicle modification (PR-10) and HISA (PR-09) are SPECIALIZED programs with high dollar values and long timelines. Vehicle modifications can cost up to $80,000+ for a full van conversion and may take months from evaluation through installation. HISA involves construction contractors and home inspections. These programs require separate workflow tracking from standard prosthetic purchasing.
Rule 11: INTERNATIONAL ADAPTATION: Prosthetics/DME programs are HIGHLY variable internationally. The VA's prosthetic benefit is exceptionally comprehensive (no dollar limits except HISA). Other countries: UK NHS provides prosthetics through regional centres, Australia has NDIS for disability equipment, commercial insurance limits DME coverage. The core prosthetic workflow (prescription -> procurement -> fitting -> delivery) is universal, but eligibility rules, funding mechanisms, and item coding (HCPCS is US-specific) change by country. International deployments need configurable eligibility rules and item coding systems.
Rule 12: Tenant Profile Matrix visibility: Prosthetics = PRIMARY for Profile A (Full Hospital — comprehensive prosthetics service). OPTIONAL for Profile B (Multi-Specialty Clinic — may have prosthetics dispensing for simple items). HIDDEN for Profiles C-G (not relevant to standalone pharmacy/lab/imaging). Home Oxygen (PR-06) might be separately visible for clinics that manage oxygen patients without full prosthetics service.
