# Section A: Women's Health (WV Namespace)
## A.1 Foundation
Women's Health V1.0 (WV namespace) tracks gender-specific preventive care and reproductive health for women veterans. VA serves over 800,000 women veterans — the fastest-growing demographic in VA health care. The WH package manages: breast cancer screening (mammography tracking with BI-RADS codes), cervical cancer screening (Pap smear/HPV tracking), maternity care coordination (Maternity Care Coordinators — MCCs), contraception management, and gender-specific clinical reminders.
WH UM: 'File numbers are in the range of 790 through 794 stored in the ^WV global. Patient Management manages individual patient care: procedures, due dates, correspondence. Possible to maintain date of next PAP smear, colposcopy, or mammogram, patient current PAP regimen. Track individual procedures: date performed, provider, clinic, results/diagnosis. Notifications (letters and phone calls) may be tracked. Reminder letters queued months in advance of future appointment.'

# Section B: Oncology / Tumor Registry (ONC Namespace)
## B.1 Foundation
Oncology V2.2 (ONC namespace, revised Sept 2024) provides the VA Cancer Registry System — OncoTrax. VA's National Oncology Program diagnoses and treats over 56,000 veterans with cancer annually. The tumor registry is a REGULATORY REQUIREMENT per American College of Surgeons (ACoS) Commission on Cancer and VA Central Cancer Registry System (VACRS/VACCR). ONC TM: 'VA Automated Tumor Registry written in standard M and FileMan. Four unique files: Site-Group for Oncology, AJCC Staging Groups, ICDO Topography, ICDO Morphology. These files create site-specific topography, morphology, and extent of disease coding per SEER, ACoS, and AJCC TNM conventions.'
Key standards: NAACCR (North American Association of Central Cancer Registries) data format for reporting, ICD-O-3 for topography/morphology coding, AJCC TNM staging (8th edition), SEER Summary Stage, CAP synoptic protocols (linked to Vol 6 Supplement AP), and ACoS Commission on Cancer accreditation requirements.

# Section C: Dental
## C.1 Foundation
VA Dental Service provides comprehensive dental care to eligible veterans: diagnostic, preventive, restorative, endodontic, periodontal, prosthodontic, oral surgery, and orthodontic services. VHA Directive 1130 governs the dental program. Dental uses ADA CDT (Current Dental Terminology) codes for procedure coding — separate from CPT codes used by medical services. Dental records require unique data structures: tooth-specific charting (32 permanent teeth + primary teeth), periodontal pocket depth measurements (6 sites per tooth), and dental-specific treatment planning.
VistA's dental record functionality has historically been LIMITED compared to dedicated dental practice management software. Many VA dental clinics use supplemental commercial dental software alongside VistA. VistA Evolved's dental module should provide modern dental charting and treatment planning that eliminates the need for supplemental software.

# Section D: Social Work / Case Management
## D.1 Foundation
Social Work in VistA does NOT have a dedicated software package — it operates through clinical documentation (TIU), consult tracking (#123), and case management functions within CPRS. Social workers provide: psychosocial assessments, discharge planning, community resource referrals, caregiver support, homeless veteran services, substance abuse counseling coordination, advance directive facilitation, and crisis intervention. Despite lacking a dedicated package, social work functionality is essential for care coordination, especially for complex patients.

# 4. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: Women's Health screening tracking (breast + cervical) is a PATIENT SAFETY and REGULATORY requirement. VHA mandates timely mammography screening, BI-RADS result documentation linked to radiology orders in CPRS, and abnormal cervical pathology result communication within 7 calendar days. The modern UI must enforce these timelines with automated alerts and escalation when deadlines approach.
Rule 2: The Oncology/Tumor Registry (OncoTrax) is a REGULATORY REQUIREMENT per ACoS Commission on Cancer accreditation and VHA Directive 1412. Cancer abstracts must be completed for all reportable cases using current NAACCR data standards. The abstract entry screen (ONC-02) is EXTREMELY structured with hundreds of data elements — ICD-O-3 topography and morphology codes, AJCC TNM staging fields, treatment fields, follow-up fields. The modern UI must make this complex data entry efficient for Certified Tumor Registrars (CTRs).
Rule 3: Automatic case finding is a CRITICAL feature of the tumor registry. The system automatically searches pathology reports, radiology reports, and the PTF for diagnoses meeting cancer reportability criteria and adds them to Suspense. This ensures no cancer case is missed. The modern product must maintain this automatic case finding capability and potentially enhance it with NLP-based pathology report scanning.
Rule 4: Dental charting requires TOOTH-SPECIFIC data structures unlike any other clinical module. Each of 32 permanent teeth has: condition status (sound, decayed, missing, restored, crowned, implant, impacted), multiple surface designations (MODBL — Mesial, Occlusal, Distal, Buccal, Lingual), and periodontal measurements (6 probing depth sites per tooth = 192 measurements). The dental chart UI must be graphical with an interactive tooth diagram, not a tabular list.
Rule 5: ADA CDT codes are SEPARATE from CPT codes. Dental procedures use CDT codes (D0100-D9999) maintained by the American Dental Association. CDT codes are updated annually. The dental module must use CDT codes (not CPT codes) for procedure documentation and billing. The CDT code table must be updatable independently of other code systems.
Rule 6: Social Work has NO dedicated VistA package. Its functionality is built on: TIU (clinical notes with social work templates), Consults (#123 for referrals to social work), Health Factors (for structured data capture like MST screening, homelessness status), and CPRS Care Management (for case management tracking). The modern UI should UNIFY these distributed functions into a coherent Social Work workspace rather than requiring social workers to navigate multiple disconnected VistA components.
Rule 7: Maternity Care Coordination is a GROWING VA program as the women veteran population expands. Most maternity care is provided by community (non-VA) providers and covered by VA. The MCC's role is coordination — tracking the pregnancy, ensuring appointments are kept, managing VA-community provider communication, and postpartum follow-up. The modern UI should support this coordination role with timeline views and community provider communication tools.
Rule 8: NAACCR data exchange is the NATIONAL STANDARD for cancer registry reporting. VA must report to state cancer registries using NAACCR format. The NAACCR version changes periodically (currently v24/v25). The Oncology configuration must support NAACCR version upgrades as new versions are released. ONC TM Appendix A: 'Upgrading NAACCR Versions.'
Rule 9: Advance directive status (SW-05) should be visible ACROSS ALL WORKSPACES — not just in the Social Work module. A banner or flag in the patient header indicating 'Advance Directive on File' or 'No Advance Directive — Discussion Needed' is a patient safety feature. During emergencies, care team needs immediate visibility of DNR/DNI status.
Rule 10: Discharge planning (SW-02) is TIME-SENSITIVE and directly impacts hospital throughput and readmission rates. Social work involvement in discharge planning should begin within 24 hours of admission for complex patients (per Joint Commission standards). The modern UI should auto-trigger discharge planning workflow for patients meeting complexity criteria (long expected LOS, homelessness, substance use, mental health, multiple comorbidities).
Rule 11: INTERNATIONAL ADAPTATION: Women's Health screening protocols vary by country (mammography starting age, Pap screening intervals, HPV vaccination programs). Cancer registry standards: NAACCR is North American; international equivalents include European Network of Cancer Registries (ENCR) and International Association of Cancer Registries (IACR). ADA CDT codes are US/Canadian — other countries use ICD-DA or local dental coding. Social work programs are country-specific (US VA homeless programs have no direct international equivalent). All four specialties need CONFIGURABLE protocols and coding systems.
Rule 12: Tenant Profile Matrix visibility: Women's Health = PRIMARY for Profile A (Full Hospital), OPTIONAL for Profile B (Multi-Specialty Clinic if they serve women). Oncology = PRIMARY for Profile A (with cancer program), HIDDEN for others. Dental = PRIMARY for Profile A (with dental service), HIDDEN for others. Social Work = PRIMARY for Profile A, OPTIONAL for Profile B. All four are HIDDEN for Profiles C-G (standalone pharmacy/lab/imaging).
