WF-05: Patients / Registration Workspace
Document 5 of 16 | ~12 Pages | Patient Registration, Demographics, Eligibility, Enrollment, Means Test, Admissions, Transfers, Discharges
March 2026 | The Patients/Registration workspace manages patient identity and administrative data: new patient registration (PATIENT #2), demographic updates, eligibility determination (ENROLLMENT/ELIGIBILITY #27.11), means test and copay assessment, insurance capture (INSURANCE TYPE #36), admission/discharge/transfer (ADT via PATIENT MOVEMENT #405), bed management, and patient record flags. This workspace is primarily used by registration clerks, eligibility staff, ADT coordinators, and health benefits advisors. References Vol 3 (Patient/Registration) screen contracts.

PT-01
New Patient Registration
Form (WF-01 5.3) COMPLEX
Multi-section accordion form: (1) Identity: Full Legal Name (last, first, middle, suffix, prefix), SSN (with SSN-less registration option for non-US), Date of Birth, Sex (M/F/Other per VHA Directive 1341), Self-Identified Gender Identity (per current VHA policy), Preferred Name, Veteran Status (Y/N). (2) Contact: Street Address (line 1, 2), City, State, ZIP (USPS validation), County, Country, Phone (home, cell, work), Email, Emergency Contact (name, relationship, phone). (3) Demographics: Race (multi-select per OMB), Ethnicity (Hispanic/Latino Y/N), Marital Status, Religion, Language Preference, Interpreter Needed (Y/N). (4) Military Service: Branch, Service Dates (entry, separation), Era (Vietnam/Gulf/OEF-OIF/etc.), Service Connected (Y/N + %), POW/Purple Heart/Combat Vet flags. (5) Insurance: Primary/Secondary/Tertiary (see PT-05 for detail). (6) Next of Kin: Name, Relationship, Address, Phone.
ZVE PATIENT REGISTER wrapper -> PATIENT #2 (core demographics) + linked sub-files.
SSN validation: Verify-SSN check against SSA database (or international equivalent). Duplicate detection: as user enters name + DOB + SSN, system searches for existing patients with similar data and displays potential matches (prevents duplicate registrations — a critical patient safety issue). If match found: 'Use Existing Patient' option. If new: creates new PATIENT #2 entry. International readiness: non-SSN identifier support, address format flexibility, country-specific validation.
PT-02
Edit Demographics
Form (WF-01 5.3)
Same field layout as PT-01 but pre-populated with existing patient data. Changed fields highlighted (yellow background on modified values). Change reason optional but encouraged for audit. Sections collapsible — user opens only the section needing update. Audit trail: bottom section shows history of demographic changes with dates and editors.
ZVE PATIENT EDIT wrapper -> PATIENT #2.
Most common use: address change, phone update, insurance change, emergency contact update. Name change requires supervisor approval (legal name changes have identity implications). SSN change requires supervisor approval + documented reason. Gender identity updates per VHA Directive — handled sensitively with preferred name propagating to Patient Banner.
PT-03
Duplicate Patient Management
Worklist (WF-01 5.1) + Detail View
Potential duplicate pairs list: Patient A Name+DOB+SSN vs Patient B Name+DOB+SSN, Similarity Score (%), Status (Pending Review / Confirmed Duplicate / Not Duplicate). Click pair: shows side-by-side comparison of all demographic fields + clinical data summary (encounter count, problem count, medication count for each record). Actions: Merge (designate primary record, merge secondary into primary), Not Duplicate (mark as reviewed, not same person), Flag for Manual Review.
ZVE PATIENT DUPLICATE wrapper -> Patient Merge utilities.
Patient merge is HIGH-RISK: merging combines all clinical data from two records into one. Incorrect merge can contaminate patient records (patient safety). Merge requires: Supervisor approval, documented review of both records, confirmation dialog with 'I verify these are the same person' attestation. IRREVERSIBLE: merge cannot be undone (I/L3 per Audit Matrix). Split (un-merge) requires national-level support request.

PT-04
Enrollment / Eligibility Status
Detail View (WF-01 5.2)
Top: Enrollment Status summary card (Enrolled / Pending / Rejected / Not Enrolled), Priority Group (1-8 with sub-groups), Eligibility determined date. Body sections: (1) Eligibility Determination: eligible services list, service-connected conditions + percentages, special authorities (Agent Orange, Camp Lejeune, PACT Act, MST, Combat Vet, etc.). (2) Enrollment History: chronological table of enrollment status changes. (3) Copay Assessment: copay test result (Exempt / Category A / B / C), annual income thresholds. (4) Benefit Letters: generate and display eligibility letters. Action: Re-evaluate Eligibility (triggers fresh eligibility determination from VES/HEC data).
ZVE PATIENT ELIG wrapper -> PATIENT ENROLLMENT #27.11, ELIGIBILITY #8.  VES (Veteran Enrollment System) integration.
Priority group determines access and copay obligations. Service-connected percentage affects: copay exemptions, dental eligibility, pharmacy benefits. PACT Act expansion: toxic exposure eligibility automatically applied per legislative rules. Re-evaluation: pulls latest data from VES/HEC national system. Enrollment status visible across all workspaces (affects billing, pharmacy benefits, community care eligibility).
PT-05
Means Test / Copay Assessment
Form (WF-01 5.3)
Income assessment form (annual): (1) Veteran Income: gross wages, net income from farm/business, other income, total. (2) Spouse Income (if applicable): same categories. (3) Dependent Information: number of dependents, dependent income. (4) Net Worth: assets. (5) Medical Expenses: unreimbursed medical expenses (deductible). Auto-calculated: GMT (Geographic Means Test) threshold based on ZIP code, copay category assignment, comparison to VA National Income Threshold. Result: Copay Exempt / Category A (no copay) / B (reduced copay) / C (full copay).
ZVE PATIENT MEANS wrapper -> ANNUAL MEANS TEST #408.31. IVM (Income Verification Match) integration.
Annual requirement: means test must be renewed yearly. IVM verification: VA cross-checks declared income with IRS/SSA data. Discrepancy flags for follow-up. Hardship exemption: if income drops mid-year (job loss, medical emergency), expedited reassessment. Result feeds: pharmacy copay calculations (ECME billing), outpatient copay obligations, and billing workspace (Vol 8).
PT-06
Insurance Capture
Form (WF-01 5.3)
Insurance entry for third-party billing: (1) Insurer (searchable from INSURANCE COMPANY #36 + manual entry for unknown), (2) Subscriber ID / Group Number / Policy Number, (3) Subscriber Name + Relationship to Veteran (Self/Spouse/Parent/Other), (4) Coverage Type (Inpatient/Outpatient/Pharmacy/Mental Health/All), (5) Effective Dates (start/end), (6) Coordination of Benefits (Primary/Secondary/Tertiary). Insurance verification: electronic eligibility check (270/271 HIPAA transaction). Multiple insurance entries supported.
ZVE PATIENT INSURANCE wrapper -> INSURANCE TYPE #36, IB INSURANCE BUFFER. 270/271 eligibility check via clearinghouse.
Electronic verification: real-time 270/271 eligibility inquiry to payer confirms coverage status, copay/deductible info, coverage dates. Result stored. Expired insurance flagged for update. Insurance data feeds: ECME pharmacy claims (Vol 5F), IB third-party billing (Vol 8), Community Care authorizations. Patient may have: VA + Medicare + Medigap + Private — all captured for optimal billing.

PT-07
Admission
Form (WF-01 5.3)
Admission form: Patient (pre-loaded or search), Admission Type (Scheduled/Emergency/Direct/Transfer-In), Admitting Diagnosis (ICD-10 search), Admitting Physician, Treating Specialty (Medicine, Surgery, Psychiatry, Neurology, etc.), Ward/Room/Bed Assignment (from PT-10 bed board — shows available beds with filtering by specialty, isolation capability, gender). Admission Date/Time (defaults NOW). Pre-Admission Testing status. Insurance verification prompt (if not verified within 30 days).
ZVE ADT ADMIT wrapper -> PATIENT MOVEMENT #405, FACILITY MOVEMENT #405.1.
Bed assignment: interactive bed selector showing ward map with occupied (gray) / available (green) / cleaning (yellow) / blocked (red) beds. Gender-appropriate room assignment enforced. Isolation rooms flagged with icons (airborne, contact, droplet). Admission triggers: (1) Pharmacy auto-generates admission medication reconciliation, (2) Nursing auto-generates admission assessment due task, (3) Dietetics receives diet order notification, (4) Social Work discharge planning trigger for complex patients.
PT-08
Transfer
Form (WF-01 5.3)
Transfer form: Patient (pre-loaded — must be current inpatient), Transfer From (current ward/bed — auto-displayed), Transfer To Ward/Bed (bed selector same as admission), Transfer Reason (dropdown: clinical improvement, clinical deterioration, specialty transfer, bed management, patient request, isolation requirement), New Treating Specialty (if changing service), New Attending Physician, Transfer Date/Time.
ZVE ADT TRANSFER wrapper -> PATIENT MOVEMENT #405.
Transfer creates new movement record (#405) preserving the chain: Admission -> Transfer(s) -> Discharge. Nurse Dashboard updates immediately (patient appears on new ward's assignment). Medication orders may need re-verification after specialty change. Diet orders carry forward unless actively changed.
PT-09
Discharge
Form (WF-01 5.3)
Discharge form: Patient (must be current inpatient), Discharge Date/Time, Discharge Disposition (Home, SNF, Rehab, AMA, Expired, Transfer Out — dropdown), Discharge Diagnosis (ICD-10 — multiple supported, primary + secondary), Discharge Condition (Improved/Stable/Unchanged/Deteriorated/Expired), Follow-Up Instructions (template or free text), Discharge Medications (reconciled list from pharmacy), Discharge Summary Note link (TIU #8925 — must be signed or at least dictated). Discharge Planning completion status (from SW-02).
ZVE ADT DISCHARGE wrapper -> PATIENT MOVEMENT #405. PTF (Patient Treatment File) for episode closure.
Discharge triggers: (1) Bed freed on bed board (PT-10 updated), (2) Inpatient medication orders auto-discontinued (pharmacy notification), (3) Diet order cancelled (nutrition notification), (4) Nursing tasks cleared, (5) PTF record closed for billing/reporting, (6) DRG assignment for billing (Vol 8 integration), (7) Discharge summary note required (alert if unsigned after 48 hours). AMA discharge: special documentation required (patient acknowledged risks, witnessed).
PT-10
Census / Bed Management Board
Dashboard (WF-01 5.4) SPECIALIZED
Visual ward map: grid layout showing wards as row groups, rooms/beds as cells. Cell content: patient name (bold), admit date, attending, acuity badge. Cell color: Occupied (blue), Available (green), Cleaning/Pending (yellow), Blocked/Maintenance (red), Reserved (purple). KPI row: Total Beds, Occupied, Available, Occupancy %, Pending Admissions, Pending Discharges. Filter: Ward, Specialty, Isolation capability. Actions: click available bed to start admission, click occupied bed to view patient summary.
ZVE ADT CENSUS wrapper -> PATIENT MOVEMENT #405, BED STATUS TRACKING.
Real-time bed board — the ADT coordinator's primary screen. Drag-and-drop: drag patient from one bed to another to initiate transfer (opens PT-08 pre-populated). Color-coded bed status provides instant capacity visualization. Pending admissions: patients in ED or pre-op awaiting bed assignment shown in 'Waiting' panel alongside bed board. Pending discharges: patients with discharge orders but not yet physically discharged shown with clock icon. Housekeeping integration: bed status transitions Occupied -> Cleaning -> Available tracked.

PT-11
Patient Record Flags
Detail View (WF-01 5.2) + Form
Display: all active flags for patient grouped by category. Categories: (1) Behavioral (Disruptive Behavior, Violent, Threats — RED badges), (2) Safety (Fall Risk, Suicide Risk, Elopement Risk — AMBER badges), (3) Clinical (Isolation, DNR/DNI, Allergies requiring special precautions — BLUE badges), (4) Administrative (Missing Patient, Flagged for ID Verification, VIP/Sensitive Record — GRAY badges). Each flag: type, active date, review date, assigning authority, narrative description, linked documents. Actions: Add Flag (form: type, reason narrative, review date, supporting documentation), Inactivate Flag (reason + date), Review Flag (extend or inactivate). Add form requires specific access keys per flag category.
PATIENT RECORD FLAG #26.13, #26.14, #26.15 via ZVE PATIENT FLAGS wrapper.
Flags propagate to Patient Banner (Design System 3.1 — Safety Flags zone) across ALL workspaces. Category I flags (national — serious behavioral) require national review board for assignment. Category II flags (local) can be assigned by facility. Flag review dates: system generates reminder when review date approaches — flags must be periodically reviewed for continued appropriateness. Behavioral flags: trigger safety protocols (escort requirements, restricted areas, staff notification).
