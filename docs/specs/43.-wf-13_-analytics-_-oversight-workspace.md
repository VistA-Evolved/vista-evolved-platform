# VistA Evolved
Wireframe Specifications
WF-13: Analytics / Oversight Workspace
Document 13 of 16 | ~8 Pages | Executive Dashboard, Quality Scorecard (SAIL), Performance Monitoring, Ad-Hoc Report Builder, Data Extract / Research Support, Compliance Dashboard, Data Quality Monitor, Analytics Configuration
March 2026 | The Analytics/Oversight workspace provides facility-wide visibility into clinical quality, operational performance, financial health, and regulatory compliance. It aggregates data from ALL other workspaces into unified dashboards, scorecards, and reporting tools. VistA data sources: DSS Extracts (ECX namespace, V3.0, November 2025 TM/UG) which pull from VistA databases and transmit to Austin Information Technology Center (AITC) for Managerial Cost Accounting per CFO Act of 1990; SAIL (Strategic Analytics for Improvement and Learning) which is VA's quality benchmarking system measuring mortality, LOS, avoidable adverse events, patient satisfaction (CAHPS PCMH), access (wait times), efficiency, employee satisfaction, and nursing turnover (SAIL outcomes are included in every VAMC director's performance evaluation); NPCD (National Patient Care Database) for encounter-level data across all VHA; VSSC (VHA Support Service Center) for national data aggregation; and local VistA FileMan data for ad-hoc queries. VistA Evolved ADDITION: modern ad-hoc report builder and data extract tools addressing the historical challenge that 'ad hoc queries are challenging because of the size and complexity of the VistA database' (published research). Used by: facility directors, service chiefs, quality management staff, compliance officers, clinical informatics, researchers. Does NOT require Patient Banner (facility-level). References Vol 11 (Analytics/Oversight) screen contracts.

# 1. Analytics/Oversight Workspace Navigation Structure
The Analytics workspace uses VERTICAL SIDEBAR MENU (WF-01 Sub-Navigation Pattern 2) organized into three functional groups: Dashboards (visual summaries), Tools (interactive query and extraction), and Management (compliance and configuration). No Patient Banner — replaced by a Facility Context Bar showing: Facility Name, Station Number, Division (if multi-division), Reporting Period (fiscal year and quarter), and Data Freshness indicator (when dashboard data was last refreshed from VistA source systems).

DATA FRESHNESS: Analytics dashboards do not query VistA in real-time for every page load (that would impact clinical system performance). Instead, data is extracted from VistA source files into an analytics data store (read-only replica or data warehouse) on a scheduled basis. Refresh frequency varies by data type: patient census and bed status refresh every 15 minutes, clinical quality metrics refresh daily (overnight batch), financial data refreshes monthly after DSS extract completion, SAIL metrics refresh quarterly per VHA national reporting cycle. The Data Freshness indicator in the Facility Context Bar shows the last refresh timestamp for the currently viewed dashboard so users know how current the data is.

(6) EFFICIENCY: Continuity of care index (% of primary care visits with assigned PCP), observation stay ratio, potentially avoidable complications. (7) EMPLOYEE SATISFACTION: All Employee Survey (AES) engagement score, burnout indicators. (8) NURSING TURNOVER: RN turnover rate, RN vacancy rate. (9) MENTAL HEALTH: MH wait times, MH measurement-based care (% of MH patients with standardized instrument administered), MH continuity (follow-up within 30 days of MH discharge). Click any domain card: expands to show individual measures within that domain with facility value, target, national percentile, and 12-quarter trend chart.
Layout — Trend Analysis Panel (bottom, expandable)
12-quarter rolling trend chart for selected domain or measure. Time axis: fiscal quarters (Q1 FY24, Q2 FY24, ... Q2 FY26). Value axis: score. Lines: facility score (bold blue), national median (dashed gray), top quartile threshold (dashed green), bottom quartile threshold (dashed red). Statistical process control (SPC) overlay: upper and lower control limits calculated from facility's own historical data — data points outside control limits flagged as 'special cause variation' (genuine change vs random fluctuation). Treatment/intervention markers: vertical dotted lines at dates when facility implemented improvement initiatives (e.g. 'Sepsis bundle implemented Q3 FY25') — shows whether interventions correlated with score changes.
Layout — Peer Comparison Panel (right sidebar 350px, toggled)
Compare facility to: (A) VISN peers (other facilities in same Veterans Integrated Service Network), (B) Complexity peers (facilities with same VHA Complexity Level: 1a/1b/1c/2/3 — per AD-06 complexity assignment), (C) National (all VAMCs). Data Source
SAIL metric feeds (quarterly computation by VSSC/VA national analytics), local VistA data for interim self-assessment between official SAIL releases (calculated locally from same source data), NHSN infection data (CDC), CAHPS PCMH survey data (AHRQ), AES employee survey data — via ZVE QUALITY SCORECARD wrapper. DSS Extracts (ECX) for cost and efficiency metrics per DSS 3.0 documentation.
Unique Behaviors
SAIL is THE most politically significant reporting system in VA healthcare. Per research: 'SAIL is the VA's system for measuring and benchmarking health care quality and efficiency at individual VA medical centers, and for enhancing the identification of quality improvement opportunities. To promote accountability, SAIL outcomes are included in every VAMC director's performance evaluation.' This means facility directors CHECK THIS SCORECARD REGULARLY and use it to drive improvement priorities. The VistA Evolved Quality Scorecard provides: (1) INTERIM SELF-ASSESSMENT: official SAIL scores update quarterly, but the facility needs to track progress between official releases. The local calculation engine runs the same SAIL algorithms against current VistA data to provide a real-time estimate of where the facility will score at the next official release. This estimate is clearly labeled 'Local Estimate — Official SAIL Score Available [date].' (2) DRILL-DOWN TO ACTIONABLE DATA: clicking a low-performing measure shows not just the score but the individual patients/encounters contributing to the score, enabling targeted chart review and intervention. Example: high SMR -> drill to individual deaths -> review each case for preventability -> identify systemic issues (e.g. sepsis recognition delay, code response time). (3) IMPROVEMENT TRACKING: when facility implements a quality improvement initiative (e.g. new sepsis bundle protocol), the system tracks whether the targeted measure actually improves. Intervention markers on the trend chart create before/after visual analysis. (4) StAT ALIGNMENT: the Strategic Action for Transformation (StAT) program monitors high-risk medical centers. Facilities under StAT monitoring see their StAT-specific metrics prominently displayed with improvement targets and deadlines.

(4) Unbilled Encounter Aging: average days encounters sit unbilled. (5) Denial Rate: % of claims denied by payers. (6) Cost Per Encounter: average cost by clinic type (from DSS cost accounting). (7) Budget Variance: actual spending vs budget by fund control point.

Data Source

Unique Behaviors
MULTI-DIMENSIONAL PERFORMANCE VIEW: unlike workspace-specific reports (which show one domain's metrics), this page provides cross-cutting operational insight. A facility director can see: are we meeting access targets? -> Yes. Are we efficient? -> OR turnover is high. Are we productive? -> Surgical volume is down. Are we financially healthy? -> Collections are trending up but unbilled encounters are aging. This holistic view enables INFORMED RESOURCE ALLOCATION decisions. VISN/NATIONAL BENCHMARKING: per SAIL framework, facilities are compared to peers. This page extends benchmarking to operational metrics beyond SAIL, enabling facilities to identify operational improvement opportunities by learning from better-performing peers. OUTLIER DETECTION with statistical process control: metrics are not just compared to targets but analyzed for statistical significance of variation — prevents reacting to random fluctuation while ensuring genuine trends are identified and acted upon. CORRELATION ANALYSIS: the system can overlay metrics from different domains to identify correlations. Example: 'Facilities with higher NHPPD have lower fall rates' or 'Facilities with faster lab turnaround have shorter ED boarding times.' These correlations help leadership understand which investments yield the most improvement across multiple metrics simultaneously.

NOT direct FileMan queries — addresses the published limitation that 'ad hoc queries are challenging because of the size and complexity of the VistA database' by providing a user-friendly query interface over a pre-processed data store.
Unique Behaviors
BIGGEST QUALITY-OF-LIFE IMPROVEMENT for VA analysts and quality staff. In legacy VistA, creating a custom report requires either: (A) writing M code (requires programmer, not available to clinical staff), (B) using FileMan search/print (requires knowledge of VistA file structure, not intuitive), (C) requesting a report from IRM (wait time days to weeks), or (D) using DSS national data extracts (limited to predefined extract fields, not real-time). VistA Evolved's Ad-Hoc Report Builder provides a VISUAL, DRAG-AND-DROP interface that clinical quality staff can use independently. ACCESS CONTROL: users only see data sources and fields they are authorized to access based on their role (AD-04). A billing analyst sees Billing data sources but not clinical detail beyond diagnoses. A clinical quality reviewer sees clinical data but not financial detail. This is enforced at the query layer — if a user somehow constructs a query against unauthorized data, the system returns an access-denied error. PERFORMANCE GUARDRAILS: ad-hoc queries against large datasets can be resource-intensive. The system implements: (1) query complexity limits (maximum number of joins, maximum result set size), (2) execution time limits (queries exceeding configurable timeout are killed with suggestion to narrow filters), (3) scheduling for large reports (if estimated execution time >5 minutes, system suggests scheduling rather than running interactively), (4) off-peak execution for scheduled reports (run during overnight hours to avoid impacting clinical system performance). PHI HANDLING: report results containing patient-identifiable data are treated as PHI. Reports are not cached beyond the user's session. Export files are encrypted. Audit log captures: who ran what report with what parameters at what time.

Layout — De-Identification Configuration (critical section)
De-identification method selection: (A) SAFE HARBOR (HIPAA 45 CFR 164.514(b)): removes ALL 18 HIPAA identifiers. System automatically strips: names, geographic data smaller than state (ZIP codes truncated to 3-digit prefix), all dates shifted by random offset per patient (preserving intervals but not actual dates), ages over 89 grouped as '90+', phone numbers, fax numbers, email addresses, SSN, MRN, health plan numbers, account numbers, certificate/license numbers, device identifiers/serial numbers, URLs, IP addresses, biometric identifiers, full-face photographs. (B) LIMITED DATA SET: removes direct identifiers (names, SSN, full address) but retains: dates (actual dates preserved), city, state, ZIP, age. Requires Data Use Agreement (DUA) between VA and researcher. (C) IDENTIFIABLE (no de-identification): full patient data with identifiers. Requires: IRB approval with waiver of consent or patient consent, VA Research & Development Committee approval, Information Security Officer approval. Available only to VA researchers with all approvals documented in the system. De-identification preview: sample of 5 rows showing how data will appear after de-identification transformation.
Layout — Authorization & Export (bottom section)
IRB Approval Number (REQUIRED for all extracts — system validates against IRB registry if available), Principal Investigator name, Study Title, Approved Data Elements (cross-check: does the IRB approval cover the data elements selected?), Export Format (CSV / SAS / STATA / R / JSON), Encryption (256-bit AES encryption applied to all export files regardless of de-identification level), Delivery Method (secure download from system with time-limited link / secure file transfer to approved research data enclave). 'Submit Extract Request' button: for Safe Harbor extracts, may execute immediately. For Limited Data Set and Identifiable, routes to Privacy Officer for review and approval before execution. Execution: extract runs as background job (via TaskMan equivalent) — researcher notified when ready for download. All extracts audit-logged per AD-07 requirements: who, when, what data elements, which patients (by count, not by identity for de-identified extracts), IRB number, de-identification method.
Data Source
Analytics data store (same as AN-04) with additional de-identification transformation layer. IRB registry integration (if available). Secure export infrastructure with encryption and access-controlled download. Per published research on VistA data use: DSS, NPCD, and local VistA data can be extracted 'using various specific commercial statistical analysis packages (e.g. SAS, SPSS)' — VistA Evolved provides the extraction pipeline that feeds those tools.
Unique Behaviors
RESEARCH ENABLEMENT: VA is one of the largest healthcare research organizations in the world. VistA's rich longitudinal patient data is invaluable for health services research, clinical trials, epidemiology, and quality improvement science. VistA Evolved's Data Extract tool formalizes and streamlines what has historically been an ad-hoc, IRM-dependent process. HIPAA SAFE HARBOR AUTOMATION: the de-identification engine automatically applies all 18 Safe Harbor transformations without human error. DATE SHIFTING: a critical de-identification technique — each patient's dates are shifted by a random offset (e.g. Patient A's dates all shifted forward by 47 days, Patient B's dates shifted back by 112 days). This preserves TIME INTERVALS (the gap between a diagnosis and a treatment is maintained) while preventing re-identification by date. The offset is consistent within a patient (all their dates shift by the same amount) and different between patients. COHORT SIZE ESTIMATION: researchers can test whether their study is feasible (enough patients meeting criteria) before going through the full IRB approval process. The estimate returns only a COUNT, not any patient data — this count-only operation does not constitute PHI access and does not require IRB approval. REPEAT EXTRACTS: saved extract definitions can be re-run periodically (e.g. monthly cohort refresh for ongoing studies) without re-approval if the IRB approval is still active and covers the data elements. TIME-LIMITED DOWNLOADS: export files are available for download for a configurable period (default 7 days) after generation. After expiration, the file is deleted from the server. Researchers must download within the window. This prevents orphaned data files containing PHI from accumulating on servers.

Pre-built rules: (1) CRITICAL: Deceased patients with active medication orders (deceased date in PATIENT #2 but active orders in #100 — indicates orders not discontinued at death), Deceased patients with future appointments (scheduling #44 appointments for deceased patients), Patients with active inpatient status but no current ward assignment (#405 movement mismatch), Lab critical values without provider notification documentation (CLIA violation risk). (2) MAJOR: Encounters without provider assigned (PCE #9000010 visit with no linked provider — affects SAIL access measures and billing), Missing ICD-10 codes on encounters (unbillable encounters), Duplicate patient records (similar names + DOB — per PT-03 duplicate detection), Appointments without clinic assignment (scheduling data gap). (3) MINOR: Patients without updated address (>2 years since address verification), Missing phone numbers (affects recall outreach), Incomplete allergy documentation (neither 'NKA' nor specific allergies documented), Vitals not documented within 24 hours of inpatient admission. Each rule: click to see the list of specific records failing the rule with patient/encounter identifiers for investigation and correction.
Layout — Correction Workflow (bottom panel)
For selected failing records: actions to investigate and correct. 'Assign to User' (assign the data quality issue to a specific staff member for correction), 'Mark as Investigated' (issue reviewed, determined to be valid data or corrected), 'Mark as False Positive' (rule flagged this record but it is actually correct — with explanation), 'Create Batch Fix' (for systemic issues: e.g. 'discontinue all active orders for patients who died before [date]' — generates a batch correction job requiring supervisor approval before execution). Correction audit trail: who investigated, when, what action taken, resolution.
Data Source
Automated daily scan of VistA data files: PATIENT #2, PATIENT MOVEMENT #405, ORDER #100, PRESCRIPTION #52, PHARMACY PATIENT #55, V VISIT #9000010, LABORATORY TEST #60, GMRV #120.5, HOSPITAL LOCATION #44 appointment data, PATIENT ALLERGIES #120.8 — via ZVE DATAQUALITY wrapper running as nightly TaskMan background job.
Unique Behaviors
PROACTIVE DATA QUALITY is a VistA Evolved ADDITION — native VistA does not systematically monitor its own data quality. Data quality problems in VistA are typically discovered reactively: a clinician notices a deceased patient on their panel, a billing specialist discovers unbillable encounters, a researcher finds inconsistent data in an extract. VistA Evolved's Data Quality Monitor runs automated checks NIGHTLY and surfaces problems BEFORE they impact clinical care, billing revenue, quality reporting, or research. DECEASED PATIENT ACTIVE ORDERS is the highest-priority rule because it represents a patient safety risk: if a deceased patient's medications are not discontinued, they could theoretically be dispensed to a different patient with a similar name (rare but documented). REPORTING IMPACT: many data quality issues directly affect SAIL scores, DSS cost accounting accuracy, and NPCD data integrity. Example: encounters without provider assignment are not counted in provider productivity (understating true workload) and may not be attributed to the correct PCMM panel (affecting access measurement). Fixing data quality improves reporting accuracy which improves facility performance scores. TREND TRACKING: the data quality score is tracked over time. Improving score indicates better data entry practices and process controls. Declining score may indicate: new staff needing training, software patch causing data issues, or process breakdown.
