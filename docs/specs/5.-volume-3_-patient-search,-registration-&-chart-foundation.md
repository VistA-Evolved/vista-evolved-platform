# 1. Foundation and Key Rules
This workspace handles the most sensitive data in the entire product: patient identity. Errors here — wrong patient selected, duplicate patient created, identity data corrupted — have direct patient safety and operational consequences. Every design and implementation decision in this workspace must prioritize identity safety over speed or visual simplicity.
Source: VHA Directive 1906 establishes that patient identity management in VistA is governed by specific traits (Name, SSN, DOB, Birth Sex) matched through the MPI, and that catastrophic edits to identity traits require supervisory review and HC IdM coordination.

# 3. Screen Contracts
## 3.1 PAT-01: Patient Search
### Purpose
Provide a safe, fast way to find the correct patient before any charting, scheduling, or registration action. This is the most critical identity-safety screen in the product. Wrong-patient selection here cascades into every downstream workflow.
Source: CPRS patient selection: fPtSel.pas. Patient lists: rCore.pas. Patient CWAD postings: fPtCWAD.pas. Patient record flags: SDES PATIENT FLAGS. VHA Directive 1906 governs identity trait matching.
### Layout
Page header with title and primary actions. Prominent search bar (largest visual element). Recent patients / worklist panel below search (for quick re-entry). Search results table. Warning/flag indicator column in results. Right-side detail preview drawer (opens on row hover or click).

## 3.2 PAT-03: Register Patient Wizard
### Purpose
Create a new patient record for someone applying for care. This is the most important write-action in the patient identity domain. It creates an entry in PATIENT #2 that will be referenced by every other workspace in the system.
Source: Terminal equivalent: [DG REGISTER PATIENT] option. ADT Registration Manual (Dec 2025): 'This option is used when the patient is applying for care.' The registration process uses PATIENT DEMOGRAPHIC DATA SCREEN <1> and subsequent screens depending on patient type. The latest patch DG*5.3*1111 modified Screen <1> to remove PREFIX and DEGREE fields from Data Group [1]. The consistency checker runs at conclusion of registration and places inconsistent records in INCONSISTENT DATA #38.5.
### Pre-Condition: Mandatory Search First
The wizard MUST begin with a mandatory search step. The user cannot skip directly to entering a new patient. This is the primary defense against duplicate patient creation.
DESIGN RULE: The 'Register New Patient' button should NEVER be the first thing visible. It should only appear AFTER a search has been performed and no satisfactory match was found. This matches VistA behavior where [DG REGISTER PATIENT] prompts for patient name and checks for existing matches before allowing creation.

# 6. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: Patient search must prioritize wrong-patient prevention over speed. Show enough identity fields (Name, DOB, Sex, Last-4, Flags) to allow safe disambiguation.
Rule 2: SSN must NEVER be displayed in full in search results. Last 4 only. Full SSN visible only in authenticated detail views per policy.
Rule 3: Registration and Load/Edit are distinct workflows. Do not combine them into one generic patient form.
Rule 4: Mandatory pre-registration search. The Register button must not be accessible before a search has been performed.
Rule 5: Patient record flags and CWAD postings must be visible at search, selection, and chart-entry moments. Not buried in later tabs.
Rule 6: Allergy NKA (No Known Allergies) is different from 'no assessment on file.' The design must distinguish between these.
Rule 7: Patient merge is non-reversible. The merge handoff screen must show prominent, unavoidable warning text.
Rule 8: Identity trait changes (Name, DOB, SSN, Sex) are monitored and trigger audit cross-references. The edit screen must warn about this.
Rule 9: Deceased patients get restricted actions. No scheduling or registration for care. Read-only chart access only.
Rule 10: Sensitive patients trigger access logging. The design must show sensitivity acknowledgment before displaying full data.
Rule 11: All custom wrappers must use FileMan APIs (FILE^DICN for creates, DIE for edits). Direct global writes to ^DPT bypass cross-references and break data integrity.
Rule 12: The chart shell must support progressive loading (ORWCV START/POLL pattern). Do not block the entire page while waiting for all clinical data.
