# 1. Foundation: Regulatory-Driven Medication Tracking
Controlled Substances (CS) and Drug Accountability (DA) are two tightly integrated VistA pharmacy sub-packages that manage the most heavily regulated aspect of pharmacy operations: the tracking, security, and accountability of controlled substances (DEA Schedule II through V medications) and the perpetual inventory of all pharmacy medications.
These packages exist because of FEDERAL LAW, not just operational convenience:
(a) The DEA (Drug Enforcement Administration) requires: registration of every facility handling controlled substances, perpetual inventory tracking of Schedule II narcotics with every addition/removal documented immediately, biennial physical inventory of all controlled substances, specific security requirements for storage (vaults, safes, electronic access), specific forms for ordering (DEA Form 222 for C-II, now CSOS electronic), specific forms for destruction (DEA Form 41), and specific record retention (minimum 2 years, readily available for inspection).
(b) VHA Directive 1108.01(2) (amended September 2024) adds VA-specific requirements on top of DEA: the PSDRPH security key is restricted to licensed pharmacists and Pharmacy Informaticist (ADPAC), twice-weekly vault inventories, Accountable Officer (AO) oversight, receipt verification by pharmacist signature, VistA Controlled Substance software use is mandatory for inventory tracking, reconciliation process for Automated Dispensing Cabinets (ADCs), and electronic access control monitoring for storage areas.
Source: CS Supervisor UM: 'This package provides functionality to monitor and track the receipt, inventory, and dispensing of all controlled substances. It provides the pharmacy with the capability to define a controlled substance location and a list of controlled substances to maintain a perpetual inventory.' VHA Directive 1108.01(2): 'The AO must verify that receipt of the controlled substance has been posted to pharmacy inventory in VistA.' DEA 2025: Civil penalties up to $470,640 per violation.
INTERNATIONAL ADAPTATION: DEA regulations are US-specific. For international deployment, the controlled substance tracking framework must be adaptable to local equivalents: UK Misuse of Drugs Act (Schedule 1-5), EU member state narcotics laws, Australian Standard for the Uniform Scheduling of Medicines and Poisons (S2-S8), etc. The VistA CS data structures (vault inventory, perpetual tracking, inspection, destruction) are universal concepts — only the specific schedules, forms, and regulatory authorities change.

Continuous real-time tracking of every controlled substance quantity: every receipt increments, every dispense decrements, every destruction decrements, every transfer adjusts both source and destination. DEA requires this for Schedule II. VHA Directive extends to all schedules.

Controlled destruction of expired, damaged, or excess controlled substances. Requires DEA Form 41, dual-witness, and specific destruction documentation. CS Supervisor UM: 'DEA Form 41 Destroyed Drugs Report.'
Tracked in CS files. Destruction record includes drug, quantity, method of destruction, witnesses, date. DEA Form 41 generation.
CS Supervisor UM

## 3.3 CS-04: Vault Inspection
### Purpose
Conduct physical inventory inspection of a controlled substance vault. The inspector physically counts every drug in the vault and compares against the perpetual inventory record. Discrepancies are flagged for investigation. Supports barcode TRAKKER scanning for efficient counting.
### Inspection Workflow
Step
Action
System Support
Notes
## 1. Select Vault
Inspector selects which NAOU to inspect

## 2. Download to Scanner (optional)
If using barcode TRAKKER: download vault inventory to scanning device. CS Inspector UM: 'download an IRL program and the vault drug balances to the TRAKKER.'
ZVE CS TRAKKER DOWNLOAD. Prepares device with expected drug list.
Barcode scanning is faster and more accurate than manual counting. For web client: consider web-based barcode scanning as alternative to TRAKKER.
## 3. Count Each Drug
Inspector physically counts each drug. For each: scan barcode (or manually identify), enter count.
For TRAKKER: scan drug barcode, enter count on device. For manual/web: enter drug name and count in inspection form.
Must count EVERY drug in vault, not just spot-check.
## 4. Upload Counts
Upload scanned/entered counts to VistA for comparison. CS Inspector UM: 'Send Inspections Inventory TRAKKER Data to DHCP.'
ZVE CS TRAKKER UPLOAD or ZVE CS INSPECTION SUBMIT. Compares entered counts against #58.8 perpetual inventory.
System calculates discrepancies automatically.
## 5. Review Discrepancies
System shows: Drug, Expected Quantity (from #58.8), Actual Count (from inspection), Discrepancy (+/- quantity).
Discrepancy report displayed. Each discrepancy requires investigation.
ZERO discrepancies = clean inspection. Any discrepancy = action required.
## 6. Resolve or Escalate
For each discrepancy: recount, investigate, adjust (if authorized), or escalate to supervisor/AO. CS Inspector UM: 'holders of PSJ RPHARM and/or PSDMGR keys have ability to override discrepancies.'

## 7. Sign Inspection
Inspector signs completed inspection electronically. CS Inspector UM: inspector badge scanning for identification.
Electronic signature. Inspection record stored with date, inspector, vault, all counts, discrepancies, resolutions.
Legal record. Subject to regulatory audit.

# 5. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: PERPETUAL INVENTORY IS LEGALLY REQUIRED for Schedule II controlled substances. Every receipt, dispense, destruction, transfer, and adjustment MUST create a transaction record in #58.81. Balance in #58.8 must always equal: starting balance + receipts - dispenses - destructions +/- adjustments +/- transfers.
Rule 2: Every balance adjustment requires a documented reason and creates an entry in CS CORRECTION LOG #58.87. Balance adjustments without documentation violate DEA regulations.
Rule 3: Green Sheets are legal chain-of-custody documents. The dispensing pharmacist, delivering technician, and receiving nurse must all be documented. Discrepancies must be investigated and resolved.
Rule 4: Vault inspections compare physical count to perpetual inventory. ANY discrepancy for Schedule II drugs requires investigation. Unresolved discrepancies may require DEA loss/theft reporting per VHA Directive 1108.01(2).
Rule 5: Drug destruction requires DEA Form 41, dual-witness, and specific documentation of destruction method. The modern UI must generate DEA Form 41 data and require two authorized signatures.
Rule 6: Receipt of controlled substances requires dual signature: pharmacist and Accountable Officer (or designee). VHA Directive: 'Both employees must sign and date the invoice or packing slip to indicate receipt. A pharmacist must be one of the employees signing.'
Rule 7: The PSDMGR key controls supervisory CS functions. Holders can override discrepancies, adjust balances, and transfer between vaults. This key list should be monitored routinely. CS Inspector UM: 'The list requires routine monitoring and validation for accuracy.'
Rule 8: Drug Accountability interfaces with GIP (Volume 9 Supply) for non-controlled drugs and with Prime Vendor systems for electronic invoice processing. DA UM: 'Interfacing with the Generic Inventory Package (GIP) and the prime vendors invoice data.'
Rule 9: NDC (National Drug Code) matching is critical for receipt processing. The system must match invoice NDC to DRUG #50 NDC field, fall back to National Drug File #50.6 if no direct match, and allow manual selection as last resort.
Rule 10: Stock and reorder levels are set per drug per pharmacy location. When a drug balance drops to the reorder level, an automated notification fires to PSA ORDERS key holders via mail message.
Rule 11: The Barcode TRAKKER is a legacy handheld device for vault inspections. The modern web client should offer web-based barcode scanning (via keyboard-wedge or camera) as an alternative to the TRAKKER, while still supporting TRAKKER integration for sites that have the hardware.
Rule 12: CS Monitoring reports (diversion detection) are CRITICAL for patient safety and regulatory compliance. The 'CS Rxs by Same Person' report identifies potential diversion (staff member both prescribing and dispensing). The 'Partial Request for CS Rxs' identifies unusual partial-fill patterns. These reports should be prominently accessible.
Rule 13: INTERNATIONAL ADAPTATION: DEA schedules (C-II through C-V) are US-specific. The controlled substance tracking framework must support configurable schedule definitions for international deployment: UK Misuse of Drugs Act schedules, EU classifications, Australian S2-S8, etc. The core tracking mechanism (perpetual inventory, inspection, destruction, chain of custody) is universal.
Rule 14: All controlled substance transactions must have electronic signatures for audit purposes. VHA Directive: 'Access to pharmacy controlled substances storage areas is monitored through an electronic access control system.'
