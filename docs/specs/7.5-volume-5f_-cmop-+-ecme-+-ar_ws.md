# Section A: CMOP — Consolidated Mail Outpatient Pharmacy
## A.1 CMOP Architecture
CMOP is the VA's centralized mail-order pharmacy system — the LARGEST government mail-order pharmacy operation in the world. 7 regional CMOPs (Chelmsford MA, North Charleston SC, Murfreesboro TN, Hines IL, Leavenworth KS, Lancaster TX, Tucson AZ) process over 120 million prescriptions annually, fulfilling approximately 80-84% of all VA outpatient prescriptions. Each CMOP uses highly automated dispensing systems with a turnaround time of approximately two days from order to patient mailbox.
CMOP TM: 'Data is loaded into CMOP database files and transmitted to vendor automated dispensing equipment for processing and mailing.' Prescriptions flow from local VA Medical Centers to regional CMOPs via HL7 through MailMan. After filling, CMOP transmits back to the VAMC: date dispensed, NDC (National Drug Code), lot number, expiration date. The VAMC then releases the prescription and generates any copayment. Wikipedia: 'CMOP fills continuation of therapy or refill prescriptions along with non-medicine items on the VA formulary.'

# Section B: ECME — Electronic Claims Management Engine
## B.1 ECME Architecture
ECME (also called ePharmacy) is the real-time electronic pharmacy claims processing system. When a prescription is dispensed (locally or via CMOP), ECME submits an electronic claim to the veteran's third-party insurance payer using NCPDP (National Council for Prescription Drug Programs) D.0 format. The payer responds in real-time with: payable (claim accepted), rejected (with reject reason codes), or DUR warnings (Drug Utilization Review — potential drug interactions, therapeutic duplication, refill too soon).
ECME UM (Dec 2025): 'ECME allows pharmacy claims processing staff to submit, resubmit, and reverse electronic claims. Derived from the Point of Sale (POS) Application developed by the Indian Health Service (IHS), assigned to BPS namespace.' ECME integrates with: Outpatient Pharmacy V7.0 (prescription data), Integrated Billing V2.0 (creates bills for payable claims), Accounts Receivable V4.5 (tracks receivables), and CMOP V2.0 (claims for CMOP-dispensed Rx).
ECME IS CRITICAL FOR REVENUE: third-party pharmacy billing recovers significant revenue for the VA from veterans' private insurance. Every payable claim generates a bill in IB/AR automatically. Every rejected claim requires OPECC (Outpatient Pharmacy Electronic Claims Coordinator) intervention to resolve — either correcting data and resubmitting, or reversing the claim.

# Section C: AR/WS — Automatic Replenishment / Ward Stock
## C.1 AR/WS Architecture
AR/WS manages the inventory of medications stocked on inpatient nursing units (ward stock). Wards maintain a supply of commonly used medications (acetaminophen, ibuprofen, antacids, common IV fluids, emergency medications) that nurses can administer without waiting for individual patient-specific dispensing from the pharmacy. AR/WS automates the replenishment cycle: inventory levels on the ward are monitored, and when stock falls below the reorder point, a replenishment order is generated to the pharmacy.
AR/WS interacts with: Inpatient Pharmacy (Vol 5B — source of dispensed medications), Drug file #50 (formulary), Controlled Substances (Vol 5D — CS ward stock has additional DEA requirements), and Supply Chain/IFCAP (Vol 9 — for financial tracking of ward stock costs).

# 5. Rules for Design and Engineering
Rule 1: CMOP is a CENTRALIZED NATIONAL OPERATION — the modern product interfaces with CMOP, it does NOT replace it. The 7 regional CMOPs are massive automated facilities with vendor dispensing equipment. VistA Evolved handles the LOCAL side: suspending Rx to CMOP queue, tracking transmission/acknowledgement, viewing dispensing status, managing not-dispensed returns. The CMOP host facilities run their own CMOP server software.
Rule 2: ECME claim submission must be INTEGRATED into the Outpatient Pharmacy dispensing workflow (Vol 5). When a pharmacist releases a prescription, ECME automatically submits the claim — the pharmacist should NOT need to separately manage claims. The OPECC (Outpatient Pharmacy Electronic Claims Coordinator) only intervenes when claims are REJECTED. The modern UI should make reject resolution a streamlined workflow with clear reject reasons and corrective actions.
Rule 3: ECME-CMOP INTEGRATION is critical: ePharmacy claims must be submitted BEFORE prescriptions are transmitted to CMOP. If a payer rejects the claim (DUR, Refill Too Soon), the Rx must NOT be sent to CMOP until the reject is resolved. This prevents CMOP from filling a prescription that will not be reimbursed. The modern UI must show ePharmacy claim status alongside CMOP queue status.
Rule 4: NCPDP D.0 is the HIPAA-mandated standard for electronic pharmacy claims. All pharmacy claims must use NCPDP D.0 format. The standard defines: claim segments (patient, insurance, prescriber, pharmacy, drug, pricing), response segments (payable amount, reject codes, DUR messages), and reversal segments. ECME handles the format translation — the modern UI just needs to display the claim data and responses in a user-friendly way.
Rule 5: DUR (Drug Utilization Review) warnings from payers are PATIENT SAFETY information. When a payer's DUR system detects a potential drug interaction, therapeutic duplication, or excessive quantity, the warning must be displayed to the pharmacist for clinical review. The pharmacist can override with a documented reason code, but the warning must NOT be automatically suppressed. This is parallel to VistA's own order check system but from the payer's perspective.
Rule 6: Ward Stock inventory management (AR/WS) must track CONTROLLED SUBSTANCE ward stock separately with additional DEA accountability requirements. CS ward stock requires: perpetual inventory counts, discrepancy investigation, witness signatures for CS administration from ward stock, and reconciliation reports. This integrates with Vol 5D (Controlled Substances) CS accountability.
Rule 7: CMOP prescription tracking (CM-03) has a PATIENT-FACING component via My HealtheVet. Veterans can track their mail-order prescription status online. The modern product should expose this tracking data through the FHIR API (Phase 2, Artifact 9) for patient portal integration. The tracking data includes: prescription status, shipping carrier, tracking number, estimated delivery date.
Rule 8: CMOP DRUG MARKING (CM-05) determines which drugs are eligible for CMOP dispensing. When a drug is marked for CMOP in DRUG #50, the GENERIC NAME and DISPENSE UNIT fields are LOCKED to prevent mismatches between local and CMOP data. The drug must be unmarked before editing these fields, then re-marked after editing. The modern UI must enforce this lock/unlock workflow.
Rule 9: ECME AUTO-REVERSE is a configurable safety feature: if a payable claim is not confirmed within a set number of days, ECME automatically reverses it to prevent billing for prescriptions that may not have been dispensed. The auto-reverse parameter is site-configurable. The modern UI should display auto-reverse countdown and allow manual intervention.
Rule 10: OPECC PRODUCTIVITY is a key performance metric: how quickly are rejected claims resolved? Resolution time directly impacts revenue recovery. The modern UI should display OPECC productivity dashboards: open rejects by age, resolution rate, revenue recovered, reject rates by payer (identifying problematic payers).
Rule 11: INTERNATIONAL ADAPTATION: CMOP is a VA-SPECIFIC national program with no direct international equivalent. However, the concept of centralized mail-order pharmacy dispensing exists in many countries (UK NHS mail order, Australian PBS mail order). The modern product should separate the CMOP-specific integration (US VA only) from the general mail-order concept (potentially applicable internationally). ECME/NCPDP is US-specific; international pharmacy claims use different standards (UK NHS BSA, Australia PBS Online). The claims engine should be abstractable to support country-specific pharmacy billing protocols.
Rule 12: Tenant Profile Matrix visibility: CMOP = PRIMARY for Profile A (Full Hospital) and Profile D (Standalone Pharmacy — may participate in CMOP). ECME = PRIMARY for Profile A and Profile D (any pharmacy billing third-party insurance). AR/WS = PRIMARY for Profile A (inpatient wards). HIDDEN for Profiles B, C, F, G (no inpatient pharmacy operations). OPTIONAL for Profile E (Inpatient Pharmacy/BCMA — may use ward stock).
